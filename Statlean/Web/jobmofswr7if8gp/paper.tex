\begin{lemma}[Shao Lemma 3.2 — second moment of the U-statistic degenerate limit]
\label{lem:shao_3_2}
Let $Y = \dfrac{m(m-1)}{2} \sum_{j=1}^{\infty} \lambda_j \, (\chi_{1j}^2 - 1)$,
where $\chi_{1j}^2$ are i.i.d. random variables having the chi-square distribution
$\chi_1^2$, and $\lambda_j$'s are constants satisfying
$\sum_{j=1}^{\infty} \lambda_j^2 = \zeta_2 < \infty$. Then
\[
E Y^2 = \frac{m^2 (m-1)^2}{2}\, \zeta_2 .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Define
\[
Y_k = \frac{m(m-1)}{2} \sum_{j=1}^{k} \lambda_j \, (\chi_{1j}^2 - 1), \quad k = 1, 2, \dots
\]
It can be shown (exercise) that $\{Y_k^2\}$ is uniformly integrable. Since
$Y_k \to_d Y$ as $k \to \infty$, we have $\lim_{k \to \infty} E Y_k^2 = E Y^2$
(Theorem 1.8(viii)). Since $\chi_{1j}^2$'s are independent chi-square random
variables with $E \chi_{1j}^2 = 1$ and $\mathrm{Var}(\chi_{1j}^2) = 2$,
$E Y_k = 0$ for any $k$ and
\[
E Y_k^2 = \frac{m^2 (m-1)^2}{4} \sum_{j=1}^{k} \lambda_j^2 \, \mathrm{Var}(\chi_{1j}^2)
       = \frac{m^2 (m-1)^2}{4} \left(2 \sum_{j=1}^{k} \lambda_j^2\right)
       \to \frac{m^2 (m-1)^2}{2}\, \zeta_2 .
\]
\end{proof}
